Networking & Connections

What Is Networking?

Networking is the process of building and maintaining professional relationships. It involves connecting with peers, alumni, and professionals in your field to learn, grow, and discover opportunities.

Networking often begins in informal settings such as orientation events, classes, and student gatherings.

Why Is Networking Important?

  • Access Opportunities: Many job openings and internships are filled through referrals and connections rather than traditional applications.
  • Gain Insights: Networking allows you to learn about industry trends, company cultures, and career paths from those with firsthand experience.
  • Build Support Systems: A strong professional network can provide mentorship, advice, and encouragement throughout your career journey.
  • Enhance Your Skills: Engaging with others helps improve communication, interpersonal, and professional skills.

Networking Tips for UMSI Students

  • Start Early: Begin building your network as soon as you join UMSI. Attend events, join student organizations, and connect with classmates.
  • Be Genuine: Approach networking with authenticity. Focus on building real relationships rather than just collecting contacts.
  • Prepare Your Elevator Pitch: Have a brief introduction ready that highlights who you are, what you're studying, and your career interests.
  • Follow Up: After meeting someone, send a thank-you email or connect on LinkedIn to maintain the relationship.
  • Utilize UMSI Resources: Take advantage of UMSI's career services(CDO), alumni network(UCAN), and events to expand your connections.
